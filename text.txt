1 若不是他在面包房附近又碰到那群批斗篷的人，他早就把他们忘了。
2 这个小老头打了个趔趄，差点儿摔倒。“对不起。”德思礼先生嘟哝说。
3 他心乱如麻，连忙朝自己的汽车跑过去，开车回家。他希望这一切只是幻象，他从来没有幻想过什么，因为他根本不赞同幻想。
4 微风拂动着女贞路两旁整洁的树篱，街道在漆黑的天空下寂静无声，一尘不染，谁也不会想到这里会发生骇人听闻的事情。
5 He dashed back across the road, hurried up to his office, snapped at his  secretary not to disturb him, seized his telephone, and had almost  finished dialing his home number when he changed his mind.
6 He'd forgotten all about the people in cloaks until he passed a group of  them next to the baker's.
7 "Sorry," he grunted, as the tiny old man stumbled and almost fell. 
8 He was rattled. He hurried to his car and set off for home, hoping he was imagining things, which he had never hoped before, because he  didn't approve of imagination.  
9 A breeze ruffled the neat hedges of Privet Drive, which lay silent and tidy under the inky sky, the very last place you would expect  astonishing things to happen.
